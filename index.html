The problem

The money is there.
The science is there.
The coordination isn't.

Go into any watershed under ecological pressure and you will find the same story. One group is planting trees funded by a European foundation. Another is negotiating a water agreement with farmers. A third is running a carbon credit scheme that has nothing to do with either.

Everyone is working hard. Nobody is working together. Capital keeps flowing to what looks good in a grant report rather than to what the landscape actually needs.

This is a coordination problem.

🌱

🤝

📊

The solution

BIOMAS is the coordination layer that living systems have always needed and never had

We don't implement projects. We don't own land. We don't deploy capital directly. We build the infrastructure that makes everything else possible.

The operating system metaphor
Think about what an operating system does for a computer.
It doesn't run the applications — it creates the environment
where all the applications can work together.

That is what BIOMAS does for a watershed.
Layer 1 · Foundation
Bioregional Data Intelligence
The living memory of the watershed
Habitat conditions, water flows, biodiversity, social dynamics, governance relationships, economic activity — all organized through a scientific framework that makes the dynamics of a living system visible.
Layer 2
Bioregional Resource Planning
Turning data into real decisions
Water allocation, land use, biodiversity investments, infrastructure — all planned together and within ecological limits, rather than decided in isolation by actors who cannot see what the others are doing.
Layer 3
Multi-Agent System
Where coordination becomes action
Local actors and digital decision tools work together to continuously update their understanding and coordinate action. Everyone is looking at the same data. This is where real bioregional intelligence emerges.
Layer 4
Bioregional Financing Facility
Capital following ecological truth
Once shared intelligence, coordinated planning, and working governance are in place, capital no longer has to guess where it should go. It follows the priorities surfaced by the living system itself.

Current pilot

Atlantic Forest,
Bahia, Brazil

BIOMAS is currently being developed in the Atlantic Forest region of Bahia — one of the most biodiverse and ecologically pressured landscapes on Earth.

The pilot territories of Piracanga and Serra Grande serve as living laboratories for the full BIOMAS model, integrating community census data, geospatial intelligence, and the GPTLEM territorial framework.
