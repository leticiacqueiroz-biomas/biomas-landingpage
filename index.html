The problem

The money is there.
The science is there.
The coordination isn't.

Go into any watershed under ecological pressure and you will find the same story. One group is planting trees funded by a European foundation. Another is negotiating a water agreement with farmers. A third is running a carbon credit scheme that has nothing to do with either.

Everyone is working hard. Nobody is working together. Capital keeps flowing to what looks good in a grant report rather than to what the landscape actually needs.

Not because we lack solutions — but because no one sees the system they belong to.

This is a coordination problem. And it has always had a solution.

The solution

The operating system that enables bioregions to think, plan, and evolve like living systems

BIOMAS doesn't implement projects, own land, or deploy capital directly. It builds the shared intelligence infrastructure that makes everything else possible — Sense. Plan. Act.

The operating system metaphor
Think about what an operating system does for a computer.
It doesn't run the applications — it creates the environment
where all the applications can work together.

That is what BIOMAS does for a watershed.

Current pilot

Atlantic Forest,
Bahia, Brazil

BIOMAS is currently being developed in the Atlantic Forest region of Bahia — one of the most biodiverse and ecologically pressured landscapes on Earth.

The pilot territories of Piracanga and Serra Grande serve as living laboratories for the full BIOMAS model, integrating community census data, geospatial intelligence, and the GPTLEM territorial framework.
